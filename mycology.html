Concept of Mycology

To acquire more knowledge in the field of mycology, specifically fungal pathogenesis and to stay connected with academics, I started my Ph.D. in Professor. Dr. Reinhard Fischer group at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology (KIT). My Ph.D. thesis work focused on understanding the mechanism of light responses in the plant pathogen A. alternata. A. alternata is a black spore producing fungus capable of producing over 70 classes of toxins harmful to human health. I investigated the role of the red (FphA) and blue (LreA) light photoreceptors and the interplay with the high-osmolarity glycerol (HOG) mitogen-activated protein (MAP) kinase pathway. Loss- of function mutations for fphA, lreA, and hogA were created using the CRISPR-Cas9 technology. Part of the findings of this study, which has been published in mBio, showed that high-osmolarity sensing required FphA and LreA, suggesting a complex cross talk between light and stress signaling.
